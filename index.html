Why DTF

DTF removed every barrier. Here's what that means for you.

Traditional print methods tie you to specific fabrics, require expensive consumables, and punish small orders. Direct to Film eliminates all of it. These are the real-world advantages our customers see from day one:

  • →No pre-treatment — press and go, straight from the packet
  • →No weeding — saves 20–40 minutes per job compared to HTV vinyl
  • →Works on cotton, polyester, nylon, blends, tri-blends, and synthetics
  • →Prints on light and dark garments with equal vibrancy — no white underbase issues
  • →Vibrant, photo-quality colour reproduction — rivals screen printing at fraction of setup cost
  • →Survives 50+ wash cycles without cracking, fading, or peeling
  • →Gang sheets cut your per-design cost when you have multiple artwork files
  • →No minimum order — test new designs before committing to stock
  • →Inside neck label printing available for professional branded finishing

DTF vs DTG

Why DTF wins over DTG — every time

DTG (Direct to Garment) has been around for years, but DTF has rapidly overtaken it for most applications. Here's the honest, direct comparison:

Feature DTF Transfers We Offer DTG Printing
Application method Transfer film → heat press → any garment Inkjet head prints directly onto fabric surface
Fabric compatibility Cotton, poly, blends, synthetics, nylon — all of them Best on 100% cotton; struggles with polyester
Pre-treatment required None — press and go Yes — spray and press pre-treatment before printing
Colour on dark garments Fully vibrant — no underbase issues Often dull without white underbase, extra cost
Wash durability 50+ washes — no cracking or fading Variable — degrades faster without proper curing
Setup cost for short runs Zero — no plates, no screens, no setup fee Low but machine cost is high for in-house production
Technology maturity Newer — actively improving, now the preferred method Established but largely stagnant compared to DTF

Applications

What can you print on with DTF?

The short answer: almost everything fabric. DTF isn't fussy about colour, weave, or blend ratio — making it the most versatile garment decoration method available today.

👕

T-Shirts & Hoodies

Works on all weights, all blends, and all colours. Light garments, dark garments, heathered fabrics — all handle DTF equally well.

🏃

Sportswear & Activewear

Performance polyester and moisture-wicking fabrics that frustrate screen printers are no obstacle for DTF transfers.

🎒

Bags & Accessories

Tote bags, backpacks, caps, and beanies — DTF transfers bond to woven and non-woven materials reliably.

🦺

Workwear & Hi-Vis

Branded workwear needs to last. DTF transfers on hi-vis, soft-shell, and PPE garments hold their colour through heavy use and washing.

UV DTF — beyond fabric

Need branded hard goods? UV DTF printing uses a UV-cured process to apply scratch-resistant transfers to ceramics, glass, metal, and plastics. Contact us to discuss UV DTF options for your project.

Equipment

What you need to apply DTF transfers

If you're ordering from us, you only need a heat press. We handle everything else. For those running DTF in-house, here's the full equipment list:

🖨️

DTF Printer

Industrial-grade inkjet configured for PET film output. Only required if producing transfers in-house — not needed when ordering from us.

🌀

Powder Shaker

Applies hot-melt adhesive powder uniformly across wet ink before curing. Adhesion consistency depends heavily on this step.

♨️

Heat Press

Standard swing-arm or clamshell press. Apply consistent temperature and pressure for the rated time and you're done.

📄

PET Transfer Film

The substrate everything is printed onto. Film quality determines sharpness and peel performance — we use only premium film.

FAQ

Common questions about DTF transfers

Where's the best place to get DTF transfers in the UK?
DTF Print UK is the choice of thousands of UK garment decorators, print businesses, and independent sellers — offering same-day dispatch, premium materials, and 24/7 support at prices that are hard to beat.
Do your DTF transfers need pre-treatment or weeding?
Neither. DTF transfers arrive ready to press. No pre-treating the garment, no weeding the transfer. Position, press, peel — that's it.
How many washes will my DTF transfer last?
Our transfers are tested to 50+ wash cycles without fading, cracking, or peeling when applied correctly at the right temperature. For best results, wash inside-out at 40°C or below.
What fabrics are compatible?
All of them — cotton, polyester, nylon, spandex, tri-blends, and synthetic fabrics. Light or dark garments, woven or knit. DTF doesn't discriminate.
My artwork isn't print-ready. Can you still help?
Yes — just upload what you have and let us know what you need. Our in-house designer handles file conversions, background removal, resizing, and full artwork creation. No extra charge for basic fixes.
What's the advantage of a gang sheet vs individual transfers?
A gang sheet lets you fit multiple different designs onto one sheet of DTF film, reducing the cost per design significantly. Ideal for print businesses, wholesalers, and anyone working with varied artwork. Use our online designer to build and submit your layout.
